TOEFL iBT Preparation

Full preparation for all four sections — Reading, Listening, Speaking, and Writing — with strategies, templates, and practice.

Overview
TOEFL iBT Structure

Listening3–4 academic lectures + 2–3 campus conversations~36 min0–30

Strategies
Section-by-section tips
  • 1Read the questions BEFORE the passage — this focuses your attention on what matters.
  • 2Skim the first and last sentence of each paragraph to understand the structure quickly.
  • 3For vocabulary questions: replace the word with each option and check which makes sense in context.
  • 4For inference questions: the answer must be directly supported by the text — never over-interpret.
  • 5For summary questions: eliminate options that are minor details or contradict the passage.
  • 1Take notes while listening — use abbreviations and symbols, not full sentences.
  • 2Focus on the beginning and end of each lecture — they usually contain the main idea.
  • 3Pay attention to signal words: "importantly," "however," "to summarize," "what this means is…"
  • 4For attitude questions, listen to tone and intonation — not just the words themselves.
  • 5Campus conversations follow a problem-solution structure — identify both elements clearly.
  • 1Use your 15–30 second prep time to write a brief outline — not full sentences.
  • 2Speak at a natural, slightly deliberate pace — rushing causes pronunciation errors.
  • 3Use transition words ("First…", "Additionally…", "As a result…") to sound organized.
  • 4Being clear and direct scores better than complex vocabulary used incorrectly.
  • 5If you make a mistake, keep going — never restart or apologize mid-response.
  • 1Task 1: Take notes on the reading BEFORE you listen to the lecture — compare both after.
  • 2Organize Task 1 by the 3 lecture points — not by the reading's structure.
  • 3Task 2: Read both student posts carefully before writing your own response.
  • 4Always write a clear topic sentence at the start of your Task 2 response.
  • 5Aim for grammatical variety — mix simple, compound, and complex sentences.
Templates
Speaking & Writing frameworks
🎙️

Speaking Task 1 — Independent (45 sec)

Position (5–8 sec): "I [agree/disagree] that… because…"

Reason 1 (15 sec): "First, [reason]. For example, [example]."

Reason 2 (15 sec): "Furthermore, [reason]. This means that…"

Close (5 sec): "Therefore, I believe that [restate position]."
✍️

Writing Task 2 — Academic Discussion (10 min)

Sentence 1: "I believe that [position] because [reason]."

Sentences 2–3: "For instance, [specific example]…"

Sentence 4: "While [Student A] makes a valid point about [X], I think [Y] because…"

Sentence 5: "Ultimately, [position] leads to [outcome]."

Study Plan
8-Week TOEFL Study Plan
WeekFocus
Week 1Diagnostic: Take a practice test. Identify your weakest section.
Week 2Vocabulary: Study 20 AWL words per day. Create flashcards.
Week 3Reading: 2 academic passages daily. Focus on vocabulary in context and inference.
Week 4Listening: TED Talks + Khan Academy. Take notes on main ideas.
Week 5Speaking: Record yourself using Task 1 template daily. Practice Tasks 2–4.
Week 6Writing: One integrated response + one discussion post daily.
Week 7Integration: 2 full practice tests under timed conditions.
Week 8Final Review: Weakest areas only. Rest before test day.
💡 Key principle: Study 1–2 hours every day rather than many hours on weekends only. Consistency builds fluency — intensity alone does not.
