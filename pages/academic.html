Writing
Academic paragraph structure
The TEEL Structure — used in academic paragraphs worldwide:

Topic sentence — state the main idea of the paragraph clearly.
Explanation — explain or define what you mean.
Evidence — provide facts, data, or examples to support your claim.
Link — connect back to the main argument or lead to the next paragraph.
Example (Engineering context):

"Renewable energy sources are becoming increasingly cost-competitive with fossil fuels. [Topic] This is primarily because the cost of solar panel manufacturing has declined by over 90% in the past decade due to advances in production technology. [Explanation] For instance, according to the International Energy Agency, the levelized cost of solar electricity fell from $0.35 per kWh in 2010 to $0.037 in 2023. [Evidence] This trend suggests that a full transition to renewable energy in industrial applications is not only environmentally necessary but also economically viable. [Link]"
