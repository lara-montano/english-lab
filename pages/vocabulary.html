Vocabulary Lists by Category

WordDefinitionExample
analyzeTo examine in detail to discover meaningEngineers analyze data before proposing solutions.
assessTo evaluate or estimate the quality of somethingThe committee will assess the impact of the policy.
concludeTo reach a decision after considerationThe study concluded that renewable energy is more cost-effective.
constituteTo form or make up a part of a wholeProteins constitute a large portion of cellular structure.
demonstrateTo show clearly by evidence or exampleThe experiment demonstrated the validity of the hypothesis.
deriveTo obtain or develop from a sourceThe formula is derived from Newton's second law.
emergeTo become apparent; to arise from somethingNew technologies emerge as a result of ongoing research.
establishTo set up on a firm basis; to prove conclusivelyScientists established a new framework for quantum computing.
evaluateTo form an idea of the value of somethingEngineers evaluate prototypes before full-scale production.
generateTo produce or create somethingSolar panels generate electricity from sunlight.
identifyTo recognize or establish the identity of somethingResearchers identified three main causes of the failure.
implementTo put a plan or decision into effectThe company implemented new safety regulations last year.
indicateTo point out or show; to be a sign ofThe data indicate a significant increase in temperature.
integrateTo combine parts into a whole systemThe new system integrates mechanical and digital components.
investigateTo carry out a systematic inquiryThe team investigated the cause of the structural failure.
modifyTo make partial changes to somethingThe researchers modified the formula to improve efficiency.
requireTo need for a purpose; to make obligatoryThe TOEFL requires a minimum score for university admission.
significantImportant; large enough to have a noticeable effectThere was a significant decrease in pollution after the policy.
theoryA set of principles on which a practice is basedEinstein's theory of relativity changed modern physics.
variableAn element or factor liable to vary or changeThe experiment controlled all variables except temperature.
WordDefinitionExample
calibrateTo adjust an instrument to a standardThe sensors were calibrated to ensure accurate readings.
catalystA substance that speeds up a chemical reaction without being consumedPlatinum acts as a catalyst in fuel cell reactions.
conductivityThe degree to which a material conducts electricity or heatCopper has high electrical conductivity.
efficiencyThe ratio of useful output to total inputImproving engine efficiency reduces fuel consumption.
equilibriumA state in which opposing forces are balancedThe system reached chemical equilibrium after 10 minutes.
hypothesisA proposed explanation based on limited evidenceThe team formulated a hypothesis about thermal expansion.
prototypeA first version from which others are developedThe engineers built a prototype to test the new design.
simulateTo imitate the conditions or appearance of somethingSoftware simulates real-world physical conditions.
sustainableAble to be maintained without harming future needsSustainable engineering practices minimize environmental impact.
velocityThe speed of something in a given directionThe velocity of the projectile was measured at launch.
WordDefinitionExample
biodiversityThe variety of life in an ecosystemDeforestation threatens biodiversity in tropical rainforests.
contaminateTo make impure by exposure to pollutantsIndustrial waste contaminated the local water supply.
emissionsThe discharge of pollutants into the environmentThe government set strict limits on industrial emissions.
infrastructureThe basic physical structures needed for operationInvesting in infrastructure is essential for economic development.
mitigationThe action of reducing the severity of somethingEngineers proposed mitigation strategies to reduce flooding risks.
renewableNaturally replenished; not depleted when usedWind power is a renewable source of energy.
