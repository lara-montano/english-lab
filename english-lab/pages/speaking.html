Templates
Response frameworks

Sample prompt: "Do you agree or disagree: university students should be required to study abroad?"

[0–8 sec] POSITION: "I strongly agree that university students should be required to study abroad because it broadens their cultural understanding and improves their language skills."

[8–22 sec] REASON 1 + EXAMPLE: "First, living in another country forces students to use a foreign language daily, which accelerates fluency in ways that classroom study cannot replicate. For example, I have colleagues who became fluent in English only after spending a semester abroad."

[22–38 sec] REASON 2 + EXAMPLE: "Furthermore, exposure to different academic environments exposes students to new problem-solving approaches. This is especially valuable for engineering students who may work in international teams."

[38–45 sec] CLOSE: "Therefore, I believe that a study abroad requirement would significantly benefit students both professionally and personally."
[0–10 sec] TOPIC: "The professor's lecture is about [main topic of the lecture]."

[10–35 sec] POINT 1 + DETAIL: "First, the professor explains that [key point 1]. According to the lecture, [supporting detail or example given]."

[35–55 sec] POINT 2 + DETAIL: "The professor also discusses [key point 2], noting that [supporting detail]. This illustrates the concept of [main idea]."

[55–60 sec] CLOSE: "In summary, the lecture demonstrates that [restate main point in your own words]."
TOEFL Writing
Writing Task Templates

📝 Task 1 — Integrated Writing

Use these sentence starters to report the reading vs. lecture relationship:

  • •"The reading passage argues that [claim]. However, the lecturer challenges this by stating that [counter]."
  • •"While the passage claims that [point], the professor points out that [opposing evidence]."
  • •"The professor undermines the reading's third point by explaining that [detail], which directly contradicts [claim]."

💬 Task 2 — Academic Discussion

10 minutes · minimum 100 words · aim for 120–150 for a higher score:

  • 1State your position clearly with a brief reason.
  • 2Elaborate with a specific example or explanation.
  • 3Acknowledge a classmate's point and extend the discussion.
  • 4Close with a strong concluding statement.
