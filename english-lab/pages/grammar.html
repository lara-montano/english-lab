Key Structures
Grammar patterns for academic English

Passive voice is extremely common in scientific and academic writing because it emphasizes the action or result rather than the person performing it.

ActivePassive
Scientists discovered penicillin in 1928.Penicillin was discovered in 1928.
The team tested three different materials.Three different materials were tested.
Engineers are developing a new prototype.A new prototype is being developed.
Researchers have published the results.The results have been published.
Use passive when: (1) the agent is unknown, (2) the agent is obvious, or (3) you want to focus on the result, not the doer.
TypeExampleNote
Defining"The experiment that produced the best results was repeated."No commas — essential info
Non-defining"Dr. Fleming, who won the Nobel Prize, discovered penicillin."Commas — extra info
Object relative"The data that we collected support the hypothesis.""that" or "which"
TypeStructureExample
ZeroIf + present, present"If you heat water to 100°C, it boils." (general truth)
FirstIf + present, will + base"If the temperature increases, the reaction will accelerate."
SecondIf + past, would + base"If we had better equipment, we would obtain more accurate results."
ThirdIf + past perfect, would have + past participle"If they had tested earlier, the failure would have been prevented."

Noun clauses are used to report findings, state hypotheses, and present arguments in academic writing.

  • •"The study shows that air pollution has increased significantly."
  • •"It is clear that further research is required."
  • •"The question is whether the method is reproducible."
  • •"What the researchers found was surprising."
  • •"Scientists argue that the data support the hypothesis."
